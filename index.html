I am a Ph.D. candidate at the Graduate School of Cologne University, a German Excellence center. I am affiliated with Cologne’s Center for Comparative Politics and Institute of Sociology and Social Psychology.

In my PhD, I use comparative approaches and latent variable modelling to investigate the dispositional and situational processes underlying ideological subscription. A summary of my doctoral dissertation project can be found here, while other current (and ongoing) projects are delineated here.

Currently, I am a visiting scholar (chercheur invité) at Sciences Po, CEVIPOF, Paris, France.

Before my PhD, I worked as a researcher at the Mathematical Institute of Leiden University, the Netherlands. I also assisted on teaching in a variety of statistics related courses at Leiden’s Faculty of Science in both undergraduate and graduate levels. Teaching materials and statistical tutorials can be found here (soon).

Also at Leiden University, I was formally educated in social psychology, political science & statistics. Detailed information are organized on my vitae.

I have collected interesting references, resources, lists which I have put together for myself on several topics related to statistics, R and methods. These can be found here.

Lastly, I worked as a statistical consultant for a number of departments at high-ranking universities. For information on this, please see here.
